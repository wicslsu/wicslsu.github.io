About

What is a Game Jam?

The Women in Computer Science(WICS) Game Jam is an event that takes place annually. Participants have 48 hours to create a video game from brainstorming to completion. Game jams are intense, exciting, and a ton of fun!

What type of games to create?

You can create any kind of game you want, and we're going to announce a theme the day of the event to provide inspiration. WICS wants to help you earn game development, team dynamic, and project experience from this event. You do not need game development experience, but experience in coding, art, or music helps! There will also be tutorials on fast game development provided the day of the event.

Who can participate?

This event is hosted by Women in Computer Science at LSU, but college and high school students of any gender can participate. Students under the age of 18 must be accompanied by a chaperone the whole time they are present at the jam site. You can come with or without a team- we'll match you up with people if you don't have one already! We also need volunteers to help out the day of the event. Register below if you're interested in participating or volunteering!

Women in Computer Science at LSU

Women in Computer Science at LSU was founded by 7 LSU computer science students during the 2015 Spring semester. Our mission is to provide an academic, social, and professional community for women studying computer science at LSU. We hold regular tutoring sessions, professional workshops, socials, and other events, all of which are open to LSU students. We're always looking for new members and sponsors; if you're interested, contact wicslsu@gmail.com.
